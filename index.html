Which blood types are rare?

A rare blood type is any blood type that is needed by patients and is difficult to find in the population.

O+

1 in 3 donors is O positive O positive is the most common blood type as around 35% of our blood donors have it.

O-

Around 1 in 7 people have O negative blood Around 13% of our blood donors have O negative blood.

A+

1 in 3 donors is A positive Around 30% of donors have A positive blood, making it the second most common blood type after O positive (36%).

A-

1 in 13 donors is A negative Around 8% of donors have A negative blood. In comparison, 30% of donors have A positive blood.

B+

1 in 13 donors is B positive This means only 8% of donors have B positive blood.

B-

1 in 50 donors is B negative B negative blood is one of the rarest blood types as just 2% of our blood donors have it

AB+

1 in 50 donors is AB positive Just 2% of donors have AB positive blood making it one of the rarest blood types in the country.

AB-

1 in 100 donors is AB negative AB negative is the rarest blood type in the ABO blood group, accounting for just 1% of our blood donors. In total only 3% of donors belong to the AB blood group.
